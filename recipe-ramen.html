Pantry Ramen

🇯🇵 Japanese
⏱ 25 minutes
👤 2 servings
⭐ Hero: Noodles

This is the ramen recipe that changed everything for me. I was craving the real thing but had none of the specialty ingredients — no dashi, no mirin, no ramen noodles, no miso paste. So I built the broth from scratch using soy sauce, peanut butter, garlic, and chilli flakes. The result was deeply savoury, warming, and genuinely good. Regular spaghetti holds up brilliantly as the noodle. This is the Mélange of Flavours approach — no excuses, no specialty stores, just flavour.

👨‍🍳 Method

Step by step — takes about 25 minutes total.

1

Build the broth

Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for 60 seconds until fragrant — don't let it brown. Add stock, soy sauce, peanut butter, and chilli flakes. Whisk until the peanut butter dissolves completely into the broth. Simmer on low for 8 minutes to let the flavours meld.

💡 Taste the broth before adding noodles and adjust soy sauce or chilli to your preference. This is where all the flavour lives.
2

Soft boil the eggs

Bring a small pot of water to a boil. Lower eggs in gently and cook for exactly 6 minutes and 30 seconds for a jammy, soft-set yolk. Transfer immediately to a bowl of ice water for 3 minutes. Peel and set aside.

💡 6.5 minutes is the magic number for that perfect ramen egg yolk. Start a timer the second the eggs go in.
3

Cook the noodles

Cook spaghetti or noodles according to packet instructions, minus 1 minute (they'll continue cooking in the hot broth). Drain and divide between two deep bowls.

4

Assemble and serve

Ladle the hot broth generously over the noodles. Halve your soft-boiled eggs and place on top. Scatter spring onions over everything. Serve immediately — ramen waits for no one.

💡 Warm your bowls first by filling them with hot water for a minute, then pouring it out. Hot bowls keep your ramen hotter for longer.

📝 Ashima's Notes

  • The peanut butter is the secret ingredient here — it gives the broth body and a subtle nuttiness without being obvious. Don't skip it.
  • No sesame oil? Any neutral oil (sunflower, vegetable) works. Sesame just adds a more authentic flavour.
  • To make this vegetarian, use vegetable stock and skip the eggs — it's still delicious.
  • Leftover broth keeps in the fridge for 3 days and actually gets better. Add a splash of water when reheating.
  • You can add any pantry toppings — tinned sweetcorn, a drizzle of chilli oil, a sprinkle of sesame seeds.

🔄 Pantry Swaps

Ramen noodles → Regular spaghetti or vermicelli
Mirin → 1 tsp sugar + 1 tsp rice vinegar
Dashi stock → Any stock cube + extra soy
Miso paste → Soy sauce + peanut butter (already done!)
Sesame oil → Any neutral oil + pinch of sesame seeds
